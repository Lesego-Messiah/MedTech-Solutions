Disaster Recovery

Introduction

Purpose: The disaster recovery plan aims to minimize downtime and data loss in the event of a disaster. It ensures continuity of hospital operations and covers preventive, detective, and corrective measures relevant to the hospital’s IT infrastructure.

Preventive Measures

  • Regular Data Backups: Automate daily backups of critical data, stored both locally and remotely. Test backups regularly to confirm data accessibility and integrity.
  • Anti-Malware and Antivirus Software: Install and update antivirus and antimalware software on all hospital systems. Conduct regular scans to detect and eliminate threats.
  • Network Security Protocols: Use firewalls and secure routers to prevent unauthorized access to the hospital’s network. Update firmware and software regularly to patch vulnerabilities.
  • User Access Controls: Enforce role-based access control, limiting access to sensitive data based on job responsibilities. Enforce strong password policies and periodic changes.
  • Uninterruptible Power Supply (UPS) and Redundant Systems: Install UPS systems to prevent data loss during power outages. Maintain redundant systems to ensure availability during primary system failures.

Detective Measures

  • Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS): Monitor network traffic for unusual activities and notify IT staff of potential breaches.
  • Regular Audits and Vulnerability Assessments: Conduct quarterly audits of security protocols and systems to identify vulnerabilities.
  • Log Monitoring: Centralize logging of network activities to detect unusual behaviors such as large data transfers or failed login attempts. Set up alerts for abnormal activity.
  • Employee Awareness and Training: Regularly train staff on security best practices, phishing awareness, and how to report suspicious activities.

Corrective Measures

  • Incident Response Plan: Create a structured plan outlining how to identify, isolate, and contain incidents. Assign clear roles to IT staff for effective response.
  • Data Restoration from Backups: Develop clear procedures for restoring data from the latest backups. Regularly test the restoration process to ensure efficiency.
  • System Recovery and Reconfiguration: Document the steps to reset affected systems and bring them back online according to hospital guidelines.
  • Communication During Recovery: Keep stakeholders informed about recovery progress, expected downtime, and alternative procedures during the recovery period.
  • Post-Incident Review: Conduct a detailed review of the incident to determine root causes, evaluate the response, and identify lessons learned. Update the disaster recovery plan accordingly.

In conclusion, these preventive, detective, and corrective measures provide a solid framework for safeguarding hospital data, ensuring IT security, and maintaining operational continuity in case of disasters.
